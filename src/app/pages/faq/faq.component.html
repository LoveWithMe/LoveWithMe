What can I expect from the first session?
This is where I get a better idea of who you are, review your intake documents and reasons for seeking therapy currently. I will answer questions, address concerns, and create a treatment plan with goals that we regularly review.
What is Informed Consent?
Informed consent is a documented process of communication between client and therapist. The client is informed about the treatment they are receiving and that I have your full agreement to proceed as planned. What is discussed during the counseling/therapy sessions between the counselor and the client is confidential unless the client exhibits/expresses threatening or harmful behavior toward himself/herself or others and it is determined by the counselor that the client is danger to himself/herself or others. In this case, the counselor has a legal and moral obligation to contact the proper authorities, the client's family if warranted, and/or the person or persons whom the client has threatened, and confidentiality will be broken.

To learn more, read the booklet titled "Your Rights when Receiving Mental Health Services in Michigan" (Michigan Dept. of Community Health) which includes information on confidentiality (HIPAA, Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act).
